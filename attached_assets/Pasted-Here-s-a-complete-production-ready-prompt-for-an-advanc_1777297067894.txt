Here’s a complete, production-ready prompt for an advanced, modern Flappy Bird game. Use this to build or commission a version that feels ultra-responsive, visually rich, and deeply engaging.

---

🎮 Game Title: Flappy Eclipse

Core Vibe: Minimalist yet cinematic – smooth as glass, brutal as ever.

---

1. CORE GAMEPLAY & PHYSICS (High‑precision)

· Bird – A stylised, animated character (default: geometric phoenix / neon hummingbird).
· Gravity – Variable, framerate‑independent (delta time). Default: -9.8 m/s² but with optional difficulty‑scaled gravity.
· Flap impulse – One‑tap gives instantaneous upward velocity + a tiny smoothing curve (ease‑out). No input lag (<16ms).
· Hold‑to‑ascend (optional alternative mode): longer tap = stronger flap, but slower recharge (fatigue system).
· Drag & air resistance – Exists subtly; bird slowly reaches terminal velocity (makes diving feel heavy, flaps sharper).
· Collision – Pixel‑perfect bounding box with a 2px grace margin on pipe edges (to reduce “unfair” clipping). Rotated colliders for pipes.
· Post‑death – Instant slow‑mo (0.2x speed for 0.4s), screen shake (low intensity), bird ragdoll or dissolve effect.

---

2. INPUT & SENSITIVITY (Responsiveness is key)

· Primary – Tap anywhere (mobile) / Space or Click (PC).
· Sensitivity tuning – Live slider in pause menu:
  · Low – heavier bird, less flap impulse.
  · High – snappy, lighter bird, faster reaction.
· Predictive input buffering – Taps within 50ms before collision are still executed (fixes “but I tapped!”).
· Haptic feedback – Light tap vibration on each flap (optional).
· Mouse wheel – Scroll up = flap (PC alternative).
· Controller support – A/X button, with rumble on collision / checkpoint.

---

3. MODERN VISUALS & ENVIRONMENT

Dynamic Sky & Lighting

· Day/night cycle based on real‑time or in‑game score (score 0–10 = dawn, 10–30 = noon, 30–60 = sunset, 60+ = neon night).
· Volumetric fog / particles (dust, glowing embers).
· Procedural clouds that drift and react to wind (slight parallax).

Pipes & Obstacles

· Pipes have soft inner glow + animated corrosion / moss.
· Variants: double pipes, moving pipes (slow horizontal drift), rotating rings, or energy gates that require precise angle.
· Distance between pipes: randomised but fair (min / max gap based on current streak).
· Pipes grow visual cracks when you pass through them (feedback).

Bird & Effects

· Trails – particle system behind wings (colour changes with speed).
· Every 10th pipe = “Focus mode” – screen edges darken, trail turns gold, audio pitch rises.
· Death effect – fragment into mempool of glowing triangles, then rebuild on restart.

Animated Foreground / Parallax

· 3 layers: far clouds, mid ground (hills / city silhouette), close ground (repeating tiles with speed matching bird’s X velocity).
· Ground has subtle bumps / puddles that reflect sky.

---

4. PROGRESSION & DIFFICULTY ECOSYSTEM

Adaptive Difficulty (hidden MMR)

· After 5 deaths below 10 points → pipes gap widens slightly or gravity reduces.
· After 3 runs above 50 points → pipes get narrower, speed increases incrementally.

Unlockable "Flavours" (skins with tiny gameplay twists)

· Vanilla – default.
· Rocket Bird – each flap gives short thrust (extreme sensitivity).
· Ghost Bird – every 10th pipe is intangible.
· Magnetic – pipes attract bird slightly (risk / reward).
· Unlocked by achievements (e.g., pass 20 pipes, deathless for 60 sec, etc.)

Daily / Weekly Ladders

· Leaderboard: best streak, total distance, fastest time to 100 points.
· Ghost runs – race against your own best run (transparent replay overlay).

---

5. AUDIO & ATMOSPHERE (Procedural & Reactive)

· Music – adaptive ambient synthwave / lofi. Layers:
  · Base calm (below 20 score) → adds beat (20–50) → adds bass + pads (50+).
  · On death – music mutes for 1 sec, then low‑pass filter sweep.
· SFX – flap (soft feather rustle), pipe pass (chime in music key), collision (crunch + reverse reverb).
· Voice (optional) – calm announcer for milestones (“Ten…”, “Twenty”, “Godlike”) or sarcastic comments on death (“Gravity wins again”).

---

6. ACCESSIBILITY & COMFORT

· Colorblind modes – 3 presets (protanopia, deuteranopia, tritanopia) – re‑colours pipes / bird / particles.
· Reduced motion – disables screen shake, slow‑mo, and heavy particles.
· Visual cue for flaps – subtle screen pulse instead of haptics.
· Assist mode (non‑competitive) – toggle “ghost collision” (pass through pipes), or “auto‑flap” (AI flaps to maintain height).
· UI scaling – buttons, text fully responsive (mobile / desktop / ultrawide).

---

7. ANALYTICS & FEEDBACK LOOPS (for developer / player)

· Post‑death screen –
  · Graph of height over time (shows when you panicked).
  · Tap heatmap – where you tapped most (too early / late).
  · Suggestion: “Try tapping 0.1s later before the gap.”
· Live sensitivity calibration minigame – tap at green prompt to auto‑tune gravity / flap force to your reflex speed.
· Crash log – records exact frame of collision (you hit pipe top at X:Y).

---

8. SOCIAL & REPLAYABILITY

· One‑tap replay – after death, long press any key to re‑enter instantly (skip menus).
· Replay editor – record your best runs, export as GIF / MP4 with custom filters.
· Shared challenges – “Beat my seed” – fixed RNG pipe pattern shareable via link.
· Live multiplayer (optional) – split‑screen race or deathmatch (who survives longer).

---

9. TECHNICAL STANDARDS (The “will run on everything”)

· Engine: Unity / Godot / Unreal (with fixed timestep for physics).
· FPS targets: 30/60/120/144hz – input polling at native rate, interpolation for visuals.
· File size – <80 MB (compressed textures, procedural generation of pipes).
· Load time – below 1.5 seconds on mobile (asset streaming).
· Offline first – leaderboards cached, syncs later.

---

10. SAMPLE USAGE PROMPT (for AI / developer)

“Build Flappy Eclipse – a Flappy Bird descendant with pixel‑perfect collision, adaptive difficulty, hold‑to‑flap sensitivity slider, dynamic day/night cycle, haptics, replay heatmaps, and ghost races. Make the bird feel like an extension of the finger – zero input lag, buffered taps, and a post‑death diagnostic that tells the player exactly why they crashed. Support 120Hz displays and colourblind modes.”
