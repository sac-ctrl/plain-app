Here’s the complete, advanced prompt for a modern Brick Crusher (Arkanoid / Breakout) game — identical in depth and intensity to the Flappy Eclipse prompt. Use this to build or commission a slick, ultra‑responsive, visually cinematic brick breaker.

---

🧱 Game Title: Crusher: Neon Fracture

Core Vibe: Kinetic, luminous, and unforgiving. The paddle is an extension of your hand.

---

1. CORE GAMEPLAY & PHYSICS (Precision first)

· Paddle – Width scales with difficulty / power‑ups (default 120px on 1080p).
· Ball – Single ball by default; can split via power‑ups.
· Physics engine – Fixed timestep (240Hz simulation), framerate‑independent.
· Collision response –
  · Paddle: angle‑based deflection (offset from centre = horizontal angle change).
  · Bricks: per‑pixel or per‑segment physics (edges can spin the ball).
  · Walls / ceiling: perfect elastic reflection.
  · Bottom death zone: ball respawn (with life loss).
· Ball spin & friction – Optional realistic mode: ball inherits paddle velocity; spin affects brick bounce angle.
· Gravity / wind – No default, but “chaos mode” adds mild vertical drift.
· Trajectory prediction – Ghost line showing ball path (toggle on/off), live updated.
· Slow‑motion on last brick – When 1 brick remains, game speed drops to 0.6x for 1s (cinematic tension).

---

2. INPUT & SENSITIVITY (Zero lag)

· Paddle control –
  · Mouse: absolute position (binds directly to X).
  · Touch: drag anywhere on screen (paddle follows finger with smoothing).
  · Keyboard: left / right arrows or A/D (acceleration curve – starts slow, ramps up).
  · Gamepad: left stick or directional pad.
· Sensitivity tuning – Live slider in pause menu:
  · Mouse smoothing (0 = raw, 100 = heavy inertia).
  · Keyboard ramp time (instant vs. gradual).
· Input buffering – If ball is dead / respawning, store the first paddle movement to avoid missed starts.
· Haptics – Light rumble on ball‑paddle collision, stronger rumble on brick break and power‑up catch.
· Precision mode – Hold “fine aim” button (shift / right trigger) – paddle moves at half speed for micro adjustments.

---

3. MODERN VISUALS & ENVIRONMENT

Arena & Background

· Neon wireframe style – glowing grids, pulsing background with bass reactivity.
· Dynamic camera – subtle zoom on ball when it speeds up, slight shake on brick breaks.
· Parallax backdrop – 3D depth with rotating geometric shapes or cityscape.
· Reflective floor – paddle and ball cast real‑time fake reflections.

Bricks (50+ visual variants)

· Health‑based bricks – Show cracks, then glowing core, then explosion.
· Elemental bricks –
  · Fire: spreads heat wave when broken.
  · Ice: slows ball temporarily.
  · Electric: chains damage to neighbours.
· Unbreakable bricks – Metallic, with sparks on collision.
· Boss bricks – Massive, multi‑hit, with weak points.
· Animated brick patterns – Rotating shields, pulsating colours.

Ball & Effects

· Ball trail – Particle comet with dynamic colour (changes on every bounce).
· Break effect – Each brick shatters into 10+ glowing fragments that fade out.
· Power‑up drop – Holographic orbs with pulsing icons, gentle magnetic pull toward paddle.
· Death effect – Ball explodes in slow‑mo, screen desaturates, then resets.

Paddle Customisation

· Shape variations (classic, curved edges, twin‑paddle split).
· Material effects – neon, carbon fibre, translucent glass with bloom.

---

4. PROGRESSION & DIFFICULTY ECOSYSTEM

Adaptive difficulty (hidden and optional)

· After 3 deaths on same level → slightly larger paddle or slower ball speed for that session.
· After 5 successful clears → next level adds 10% more bricks or introduces moving bricks.

Campaign / Arcade Structure

· Worlds – 8 worlds, each with unique brick behaviours and backgrounds:
  · Factory – conveyor belt bricks (move horizontally).
  · Crystal caverns – bricks reflect ball at odd angles.
  · Neon temple – bricks regenerate after 3 seconds.
· Boss levels – Giant brick with phases, projectiles, and weak points.
· Endless mode – Randomly generated levels, increasing speed.

Power‑up System (balanced)

· Tier 1 (common) – Longer paddle, multi‑ball, slow ball, sticky paddle, laser.
· Tier 2 (rare) – Paddle wrap‑around (ball passes through walls), gravity well (sucks bricks downward), shield (one free death).
· Tier 3 (super rare) – Screen nuke (destroys all bricks), ball shrink (tiny hitbox, huge speed).
· Debuffs – Shorter paddle, reverse controls, invisible ball (for challenge modes).

Unlockables & Meta‑progression

· Earn crystal shards – unlock new paddles, ball trails, arenas, and music tracks.
· Daily challenges – fixed seed levels with leaderboards.
· Skill rating (E‑S rank) based on efficiency (fewest bounces, no missed balls).

---

5. AUDIO & ATMOSPHERE (Reactive & immersive)

· Adaptive music – Tempo increases as bricks count decreases.
  · Beat drop on every 5 bricks broken.
  · World‑specific genres (synthwave, chiptune, lo‑fi, industrial).
· SFX layering –
  · Paddle hit: soft “thwack” with pitch variation = f(ball speed).
  · Brick break: glass shatter + reverb tail.
  · Power‑up catch: ascending chime.
  · Death: deep sub drop + vinyl scratch.
· Voice announcer (toggleable) – “Good”, “Excellent”, “Crusher!” for streaks, sarcastic “Uh‑oh” on death.

---

6. ACCESSIBILITY & COMFORT

· Colorblind profiles – 3 presets that recolor bricks, ball, and power‑ups.
· High contrast mode – Bold outlines, no bloom/glare.
· Reduced motion – Disables screen shake, slow‑mo, particle explosions.
· Assist options (non‑ranked) –
  · Ball always locks to paddle on respawn.
  · Auto‑aim (paddle gently moves toward ball’s X position).
  · Infinite lives.
· UI scaling – All HUD elements resizable, touch‑friendly buttons (min 44px).
· Audio visualiser – Subtle on‑screen rings for each bounce / brick break (for deaf players).

---

7. ANALYTICS & FEEDBACK LOOPS

· Post‑loss screen –
  · Heatmap: where you missed the ball most often (left, centre, right).
  · Efficiency: ball bounces per brick broken.
  · Tip: “Try staying closer to ball’s landing zone.”
· Live calibration – “Paddle test” – ball launches; game auto‑adjusts sensitivity based on your reaction time.
· Replay system – Watch your last run, with slow‑mo and trajectory overlay.
· Crash diagnostic – Logs exact frame of collision miss (paddle offset, ball speed).

---

8. SOCIAL & REPLAYABILITY

· Ghost mode – Race against your best run (or a friend’s) – translucent paddle + ball.
· Level sharing – Procedural level seed can be exported as a code.
· Leaderboards – Fastest clear time, highest combo (consecutive bricks without hitting paddle), most power‑ups collected.
· Co‑op mode (local / online) – Two paddles on screen, shared ball, split bricks.
· Competitive – Versus: each player has own arena; sending junk bricks to opponent on combo.
· Replay export – Save as GIF / MP4 with optional filters (VHS, neon glow).

---

9. TECHNICAL STANDARDS

· Engine – Unity / Godot / custom (with deterministic physics).
· Frame rate – Supports 30–240 Hz, input sampled at native rate, simulation independent.
· Load time – <2 seconds from launch to gameplay.
· Memory – <150 MB (streaming assets for music / levels).
· Platforms – Desktop (Windows/Mac/Linux), mobile (iOS/Android), web (WebGL with 60fps).
· Offline – Full functionality without internet; syncs leaderboards later.
