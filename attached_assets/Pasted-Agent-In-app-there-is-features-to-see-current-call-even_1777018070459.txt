Good question — let me be straight about an Android constraint and then build the strongest solution that works around it.

The honest reality of Android microphones during calls:

Android only lets one app actively capture the mic at a time. While you are on a carrier call, the dialer (system app) holds the mic; while on a WhatsApp call, WhatsApp holds it.
Since Android 10 the system can silently mute background mic recordings during a voice call. There is no app-level workaround for that — only system / signed apps using VOICE_CALL/VOICE_DOWNLINK can grab the actual telephony stream.
The workaround that does work in practice on most devices: force speakerphone on, then capture the regular MIC source. The loudspeaker plays the remote person's voice → the mic picks it up alongside your voice → both sides come through over WebRTC. We already turn speakerphone on; what's missing is that the WebRTC audio module currently uses VOICE_COMMUNICATION, which is the worst source during a foreign call (the OS often silences it). Switching to MIC is the right call here.

Honest answer to your mic question first: Android does not let two apps record the microphone simultaneously in the way you're hoping. Since Android 10, when one app holds the mic exclusively (like the Phone app or WhatsApp during a call), the OS silently silences any other app that tries to record — there is no flag, permission, or workaround that makes two apps both get live audio at the same time. The closest thing that works in practice is: force the call to speakerphone, then capture with AudioSource.MIC (not the VoIP source, which gets muted faster), so the device's own microphone picks up both sides of the call from the loudspeaker. That's what I've implemented. It will work reliably for phone calls and most VoIP apps when speakerphone is on; when the OS does silence us anyway, the web page now shows a clear warning instead of going silent without explanation.
