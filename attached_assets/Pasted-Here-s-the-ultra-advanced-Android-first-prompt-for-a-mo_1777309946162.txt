Here’s the ultra‑advanced, Android‑first prompt for a modern 2048 (the sliding number‑merging puzzle – rebuilt for touch, with fluid animations, meta‑progression, and deep polish).

---

🔢 Game Title: 2048: Merger Ascension

Core Vibe: Tactile, hypnotic, and satisfying. Every swipe moves like silk, every merge crackles with energy.

---

1. CORE GAMEPLAY & PHYSICS (Butter‑smooth merging)

· Grid – Classic 4×4 (options for 5×5, 6×6 as unlockables).
· Movement – Swipe up/down/left/right: all tiles slide as far as possible, merging equal numbers.
· Merge rule – Each tile merges only once per move. New tile spawns (2 or 4) in a random empty cell after each move.
· Win condition – Reach 2048 tile (then continue for high score).
· Loss condition – No legal moves left (grid full, no adjacent matches).
· Score system – Points from merges (value of new tile). Combo chain = no visual delay between merges.
· Undo feature – Undo last move (limited uses or unlimited via in‑game currency).

---

2. INPUT & SENSITIVITY (Swipe perfection)

· Primary control – Swipe anywhere on grid.
  · Sensitivity: swipe length threshold (short flick vs long drag).
  · Dead zone in centre to avoid accidental diagonal swipes.
· Alternative controls –
  · Tilt (gyro) – tilt phone to move tiles.
  · On‑screen arrow buttons (accessibility).
· Haptics –
  · Light pop on tile slide.
  · Stronger buzz on merge.
  · Extra buzz on merging 512, 1024, 2048+.
  · Double buzz on new high tile.
· Swipe buffer – If swipe occurs during animation, queue it (max 1).
· Edge resistance – Swiping into a wall does nothing, but haptics indicate no move.

---

3. MODERN VISUALS & ENVIRONMENT (Minimalist but juicy)

Tiles design

· Shape – Classic rounded squares, or unlockable shapes (hexagons, circles).
· Colors – Modern gradient palette:
  · 2: soft cream → 4: peach → 8: coral → 16: orange → 32: amber → 64: gold → 128: yellow → 256: lime → 512: mint → 1024: teal → 2048: deep blue → 4096+: purple → 8192+: magenta → 16384+: iridescent.
· Animations –
  · Slide: ease‑out curve, overshoot bounce (optional).
  · Merge: scale pop (1.15x then return), particle burst.
  · New tile spawn: drop‑in with ripple.
· Death effect – Grid shake, tiles fade to grey, then reset.

Background & themes

· Dynamic background – Subtle gradient that shifts with highest tile.
· Themes – Unlockable:
  · Neon grid – glowing lines between cells.
  · Paper – soft shadow, flat.
  · Glassmorphism – frosted glass tiles.
  · Dark matter – true black background (OLED).
· Particle effects – Merge creates floating numbers that fade out.

UI & HUD

· Score display – animated counter.
· Best score – persistent.
· Undo button, new game button, settings.
· Next tile preview (optional – shows which tile will spawn next).

---

4. PROGRESSION & DIFFICULTY ECOSYSTEM

Game modes

1. Classic – Standard 2048 rules.
2. Timed – 60 seconds, score as many points.
3. Endless – Grid expands (e.g., 4×4 → 5×5 after 2048) or spawns higher numbers.
4. Challenge – Pre‑set boards with obstacles (blocked cells) or special rules.

Adaptive difficulty (keeps challenge alive)

· After 3 losses in a row → spawn 2 more often than 4, or add hint system.
· After 3 wins to 2048 → increase spawn rate of 4s, or reduce undo availability.

Meta‑progression & unlocks

· Tile skins – Change visual style of numbers (digital, neon, marble, wood, crystal).
· Themes – Backgrounds, sound packs, particle effects.
· Power‑ups (earned via daily play or achievements):
  · Undo (extra uses), Shuffle (mix tiles), Clear (remove lowest tile).
· Stat tracking – Total merges, most merged tile ever, total points, moves made.

Daily & weekly challenges

· Fixed seed board – same tile spawns for all players.
· Leaderboards for highest score in 10 moves, fastest time to 2048, etc.

---

5. AUDIO & ATMOSPHERE (Satisfying feedback)

· Music – Lo‑fi / ambient / chillwave.
  · Changes subtly based on highest tile (more intense layers as numbers grow).
· SFX –
  · Slide: soft whoosh.
  · Merge: crisp “pop” with pitch = log(value) (2 = low, 2048 = high).
  · New tile: gentle chime.
  · Win (2048): fanfare + applause.
  · Loss: deep descending tone.
· Voice announcer (toggle) – “Merge!”, “Two thousand forty eight!”, “Game over”.
· Audio ducking – Respects background music.

---

6. ACCESSIBILITY & COMFORT (Android first)

· Colorblind modes – 3 presets (recolor tiles by shape + pattern).
· Reduced motion – Disables particle effects, scale bounce, screen shake.
· High contrast – Bold tile borders, high text contrast.
· Assist modes –
  · Show merge hints (glow on mergeable tiles).
  · Auto‑undo on mis‑swipe (if would end game).
  · Slower animation speed.
· One‑handed mode – Swipe zone limited to bottom half or right half.
· Font scaling – Tile numbers scale with system font size (min 20pt).
· Battery saver – Cap 30fps, reduce animations, simple background.

---

7. ANALYTICS & FEEDBACK LOOPS

· Post‑game screen –
  · Heatmap: which cells were used most/least.
  · Merge history (timeline of highest tiles).
  · Efficiency: average tile value per move.
  · Tip: “You left your highest tile in a corner – good. But you blocked it with a 2.”
· Live calibration – “Swipe test” – detect swipe length vs slow drag.
· Replay system – Watch last game move by move (slider).
· Crash log – If game detects impossible board, logs seed.

---

8. SOCIAL & REPLAYABILITY (Android integration)

· Google Play Games – Achievements, leaderboards (score, fastest 2048, biggest tile).
· Share replay – Export game as video or text grid.
· Friend challenges – Send a board state (QR code) – friend tries to beat your score from same position.
· Daily leaderboards – Live rankings.
· Clans / groups – Weekly cumulative score rewards.

---

9. TECHNICAL STANDARDS (Android performance)

· Engine – Unity / Godot / native (Kotlin + Canvas/OpenGL).
· Frame rate – 60/90/120Hz support; animations at refresh rate, logic at 60Hz.
· Touch latency – <16ms from swipe to tile movement.
· Memory – <80 MB.
· Battery efficiency – Pause when background, minimal CPU when idle.
· Install size – Under 50 MB (no large assets).
· Permissions – None (gyro optional).
· Offline – Fully offline; cloud sync optional.
